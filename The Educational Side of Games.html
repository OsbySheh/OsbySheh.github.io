Why Games can be a Benefit for your Children




To me, games are not just a kind of entertainment, but it can also be used as educational, too. There are tons of educational games out there that can actually be more effective and efficient than thick textbooks, because kids are more interested in games, they don't like huge books with tons of word in it, it's just not appealing to them. That's why games are the perfect education that the kids need, kids will get more interested in a subject if it was taught entertainingly. There are a lot of games out there that can aid your child's education, for example, Scribblenauts Unlimited, it's a game that teaches your kid how to spell and write, and in the game, if you spell a word correctly, that object will appear. The game also encourages your kid to use their imagination and creativity, because you have to use the words you know to help the town folks in the game. Educational games can be very useful in learning, it helps you to be able to spell and write accurately, solve a variety of problems and puzzles, teaches you about teamwork...etc. I can go on and on, but I think you get the idea of what video games can ACTUALLY do.



How to Persuade Teachers to let you Game in Class




Not every school lets you play educational games in class, because a lot of teachers believed that games do not help you learn anything at all, but that is 100% COMPLETELY false, and in this article, I will help you persuade your teacher to let you play games in school. Teachers do not allow gaming because they don't understand the benefits of playing them, you need to be able to prove your point that games can actually be a great source of education. Explain to them that video games are fun to play, and they're also exciting, which makes students even more interested in a subject, because it's something completely fresh, something they haven't seen that's been used in a school before, so they will really enjoy it, and would want to learn more about it. For example, there's a science game called Sokobond, and it teaches you the basics of chemistry, and how atoms bond together. The player will have to learn how to form a correct bond with chemical compounds to get past a level, which is very challenging, but it's also a pleasant game. Use these kinds of evidence to prove to the teachers that games are pretty educating, and sometimes, you can actually learn more from it than from textbooks or boring presentations.



What are the Disadvantages of Educational Games?




There are a lot of benefits for playing educational games, but there are also some flaws and disadvantages for them, too. For example, games can only teach you the basic knowledge and ideas of a subject. If you are really, really interested in a subject, and you want to learn more than the basics of it, then you should probably still take lessons or read from novels and textbooks. Games won't teach you too much about a specific topic, it only gives you a main idea of the topic. Another disadvantage for playing educational games is that your kids might get too addicted, and they will start ignoring school work, their grades will start to drop...etc. Watch out for games like Minecraft, because this is a very common and popular game among us kids. Kids get very addicted because they can join online servers and battle opponents, which you need to watch out for, because once they got into it, it's hard to pull them back out. To prevent this from happening, you have to restrict the amount of hours they are allowed to play. For example, your child is allowed to play for half an hour after his homework are all done, or, your child is allowed to play for an hour after he or she finished reviewing for an upcoming test. That way, your kid won't be so addicted in gaming, and their grades will improve!
